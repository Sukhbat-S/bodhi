## Priority Ranking (1 = pitch first as client #2)

### Rank 1 — Fuku Ramen (~25K followers)
**Why first:** Sits squarely in the sweet spot (20K–200K). Growing brand, active enough to have budget, content is clearly an afterthought. Mongolian-only captions, no reels strategy, food photography is inconsistent. The ramen category is extremely photogenic — steam, broth close-ups, noodle pulls. This is the easiest creative case to make. Not a franchise (unlike Ajisen), so they have autonomy to approve new content direction quickly.

### Rank 2 — CHILI Shabu Shabu & Sushi (~18K followers)
**Why second:** 18K followers at a premium Shangri-La Mall location means they have disposable marketing budget. Shabu shabu is interactive dining — bubbling broth, fresh ingredients, dipping moments — arguably the most naturally viral Asian food format. Their current content is sporadic and generic. Pitch: "Your product films itself; we just need to show up."

### Rank 3 — Noka Robata Grill & Bar (~13.4K followers)
**Why third:** Robata (Japanese charcoal grill) is visually stunning — open flames, skewers, smoke. They have the content potential of a premium restaurant but post like a budget one. 13.4K FB followers + only 1.4K IG suggests they've never invested in Instagram-first content. The Olympic Galleria location attracts middle-to-upper class Mongolians who are active on Facebook. Strong visual pitch: "We can make your grill look like Tokyo."

### Rank 4 — Jangsu Korean Steak House (~11K FB / 13K IG)
**Why fourth:** Multiple locations means bigger budget and higher content volume needs. Hanwoo beef (rare Korean cattle) is a prestige product — worth ~$150-200/kg — but their social content doesn't reflect that exclusivity. This is a premium pitch: "Your beef deserves content that matches its price point." Multi-branch means recurring monthly work, not a one-off.

### Rank 5 — Ajisen Ramen Mongolia (~61K followers)
**Why fifth:** Largest follower base on the list. The risk: it's a global franchise (700+ locations), so content decisions may be centralized. However, the Mongolian branch clearly manages its own FB page (Mongolian-only posts, local promos). TripAdvisor rating is terrible (#284 of 287), which means they need help — their brand has a reputation problem that better content alone can't fix. Approach carefully: pitch as "local brand story" not "social media management."

### Rank 6 — Sura Korean Restaurant (~39K followers)
**Why sixth:** Largest total audience on the list (39K), 3 locations, established since 2013. But they're already semi-active on social — which means you'd be replacing or augmenting an existing (possibly in-house or cheap) content effort. The pitch needs to be differentiated: "You're reaching Mongolians. We'll also bring you expats, tourists, and Korean food fans who search in English." Good long-term client if you can get the meeting.

### Rank 7 — Stockholm Sushi (~8.6K followers)
**Why seventh:** Family-run, beloved by expats but below the 20K sweet spot. Lower budget likelihood. However, the authentic "best sushi in UB" reputation is untapped marketing gold. The pitch: "You already have the word of mouth. We'll turn it into content that reaches people before they've heard of you." Good if you need a quick yes — family businesses often say yes faster than corporate chains.

### Rank 8 — Oriental Treasure (~11.4K followers)
**Why eighth:** Established 2011, solid reputation for dim sum. Content doesn't match food quality. The unclear cuisine identity (listed as Thai, Taiwanese, Chinese in different sources) is actually a problem your agency can help solve: "Brand clarity + beautiful dim sum photography." Mid-tier priority because dim sum is harder to make visually dynamic than ramen or robata.

### Rank 9 — HANA Japanese Restaurant (~4.5K followers)
**Why ninth:** Below the sweet spot at only 4.5K followers and very low engagement (8 people talking). Located in Monnis Tower (upscale) so budget may exist, but the near-zero social presence suggests either (a) they don't value social media, or (b) they're doing fine without it. A harder sell. Approach as: "You're invisible online. Your location deserves better."

### Rank 10 — Sen Izakaya (~2.4K FB / 8K IG)
**Why tenth:** Counterintuitively, their IG is already good — reels, styled food photography, some English. So your pitch is weakest here (they partially do what you'd offer). The FB page is weak (2.4K) which is the pitch angle. But they may already have an agency or in-house photographer for IG. Last on the list because the sell is harder and the creative gap is smaller.

## Pitch Sequence Strategy

**Phase 1 — Quick wins (months 1–2): Ranks 1–3**
- Fuku Ramen, CHILI Shabu Shabu, Noka Robata
- All have 13K–25K followers (sweet spot), no visible content strategy, and food that photographs beautifully
- Start with these to build your portfolio with fast results and case studies
- These clients are likely independently owned or small-chain → faster decisions, more creative freedom

**Phase 2 — Upsell clients (months 3–4): Ranks 4–6**
- Jangsu, Ajisen, Sura
- All have larger followings or multiple locations → higher contract value
- Use Phase 1 results (follower growth %, engagement rate improvement) as proof
- Jangsu and Sura especially need English-language content to capture tourism/expat market
- Ajisen requires careful franchise navigation — lead with local storytelling angle

**Phase 3 — Fill roster (months 5–6): Ranks 7–10**
- Stockholm Sushi (quick yes, smaller budget)
- Oriental Treasure (brand clarity pitch)
- HANA (premium location, low effort client)
- Sen Izakaya (FB-only pitch, IG already covered)

## Same-Owner Chain Flags

- **Jangsu Korean Steak House** has multiple TripAdvisor listings (Khan Tower, KB Tower, Khan-uul, Khaan Bank Tower) — confirmed multi-location chain, likely single ownership. Pitching one branch likely gets all.
- **Sura Korean Restaurant** has 3 confirmed locations (BlueMon, Naadam Center, Encanto Mall) — single brand, likely centralized marketing decisions. One pitch = 3 locations of content.
- **Nagomi & Sakura** — facebook.com/nagomi.sakura/ suggests Nagomi and Sakura Restaurant share ownership or are sister brands. Your anchor client relationship may give warm intro to Sakura as well.
- **Takesan Miso Ramen** — NOT on the top 10 list because with 162K FB followers they almost certainly have in-house content or an agency already. Flag: too big, don't pitch cold.
