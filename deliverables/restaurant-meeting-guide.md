# Restaurant Meeting Guide — Chef Relative

> Read on your phone before the meeting. 15 min prep = 10x better conversation.
> Goal: understand the business, find the pain, propose something real.

---

## The Frame

You're NOT selling. You're a relative who builds AI systems and wants to understand his business. If there's a real problem you can solve, you'll build it — and document the journey publicly. He gets real tools for free. You get a showcase + content.

---

## Phase 1: Listen First (20 min)

Ask these questions. Let him talk. Take notes on your phone.

### About the restaurantd
- What kind of food? How many seats? How long open?
- How many staff? What's the busiest day/time?
- What's the average ticket (per person spending)?
- Who are the customers? (locals, tourists, families, couples, business lunches?)

### About marketing (the pain is usually here)
- How do customers find you? (Google Maps, Facebook, word of mouth, delivery apps?)
- Do you have a Facebook page? Instagram? Google Business listing?
- Who manages social media? How often do they post?
- Do you get Google reviews? What's the rating? Do you respond to reviews?
- Have you ever run paid ads? (Facebook ads, Google ads, delivery app promotions?)

### About operations (hidden opportunities)
- How do customers reserve a table? (phone call, walk-in, Facebook message, app?)j
- What's your no-show rate? Do people book and not come?
- Do you use any software? (POS system, accounting, inventory, scheduling?)
- What's the biggest daily headache? (staffing, food waste, slow nights, complaints?)
- If you could fix ONE thing about the business right now, what would it be?

### About competition
- Who are the nearby competitors? What do they do better?
- Any restaurant in UB you admire? Why?

---

## Phase 2: Observe While You're There (10 min)

While eating / talking, notice:

- [ ] Is there a QR menu or paper menu? no qr menu 
- [ ] How's the Wi-Fi? (customers care) no wifi info
- [ ] What does the entrance look like from outside? (curb appeal)
- [ ] Google Maps: search the restaurant name — what comes up? Photos? Reviews? Hours correct?
- [ ] Facebook: search the restaurant — page exists? Recent posts? Review count?
- [ ] Take 3-5 casual photos (food, interior, exterior) — ask permission first

---

## Phase 3: Plant the Seed (5 min)

Don't pitch a full project. Just say something like:

> "Би одоо AI систем ашиглан бизнесүүдэд туслах зүйл бүтээж байна. Чиний ресторанд ямар нэг зүйлд тусалж чадах юм байвал хамтарч болно. Миний хувьд энэ нь бодит жишээ болно, чиний хувьд үнэгүй ашиг."
>
> ("I'm building tools that help businesses using AI. If there's something useful I can build for your restaurant, we could work together. For me it's a real showcase, for you it's free value.")

Then SHUT UP and let him respond. If he's interested, schedule a follow-up. Don't promise anything today.

---

## What You're Listening For (the real deliverable)

After the meeting, you should know which of these is the #1 pain:

| Pain | What you'd build | Content angle |
|---|---|---|
| **No online presence** | Landing page + Google Business setup | "How I got a restaurant from 0 to Google Maps in 24 hours" |
| **No reservation system** | Booking form → Google Sheet (Nagomi pattern) | "Real-time reservations for a restaurant with zero tech budget" |
| **No reviews strategy** | QR card that links to Google review page + automated follow-up | "How 50 Google reviews changed a restaurant's foot traffic" |
| **Social media dead** | Content calendar + auto-post from food photos | "AI-generated food posts that actually look real" |
| **Delivery app fees too high** | Own ordering page + direct delivery coordination | "How to escape 30% delivery app fees" |
| **Food waste / inventory** | Simple tracking sheet + AI demand prediction | "AI predicted Monday would be slow — saved 200k MNT in food waste" |
| **Staffing headaches** | Shift scheduler + no-show tracking | "The spreadsheet that replaced a manager's worst job" |

Pick ONE. Not all of them. The one with the most pain = the best content.

---

## Content Capture Checklist

Before you leave the meeting:

- [ ] 3-5 photos (food close-up, interior, exterior, menu)
- [ ] Voice note on your phone summarizing the top 3 problems he mentioned
- [ ] His permission to document the project publicly ("I'll share how we built this, cool?")
- [ ] His phone number / Facebook for follow-up
- [ ] The restaurant name + exact Google Maps location

---

## The Content Arc (what you'll post about this)

1. **Post: "Research day"** — "Before building anything, I sat down with a real restaurant owner and asked: what's actually broken?" (photo of you two talking)
2. **Post: "The diagnosis"** — "Here's what I found: [specific problem]. Here's what I'm building."
3. **Post: "Day 1 build"** — screen recording or timelapse of BODHI building the solution
4. **Post: "Shipped + measured"** — real numbers (reservations, reviews, traffic) after 1-2 weeks

This is the HONEST build-in-public arc. Research → Build → Ship → Measure. No shortcuts.

---

## One More Thing

Ask him if he knows other restaurant owners who have the same problems. If yes, this becomes a repeatable service, not a one-off favor. That's the agency play — one showcase → referrals → paid clients.

But today: just listen. No promises. Notes only.
