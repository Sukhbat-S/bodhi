# NAGOMI PHONE SHOOT — 90 MINUTES, GO

**Mission:** 8 shots. iPhone only. No lights, no tripod, no excuses. Landing page ships tomorrow.

---

## 1) SHOT LIST

Shoot in this order. Drop from the bottom if time runs out.

### Shot 1 — HERO: Signature Ramen (tonkotsu or shoyu)
- **Angle:** 25deg three-quarter (just above rim, looking down into the bowl)
- **Distance:** Close — bowl fills 80% of frame
- **Light:** Pendant or pass-through kitchen light from back-left, raking across the surface to catch steam
- **Focal point:** The egg yolk half (or the chashu edge if no egg) — tap-lock on it
- **Composition:** Bowl center-left, chopsticks entering from bottom-right on rule-of-thirds line, negative space top-right for headline text
- **Why:** Ramen is Nagomi's anchor. Backlit steam + yolk gloss = the one image that sells the whole site.

### Shot 2 — NOODLE LIFT (human element)
- **Angle:** 15deg, camera low, noodles rising toward lens
- **Distance:** Close — crop tight on the lift, bowl half-visible
- **Light:** Same pendant, backlit so noodles catch highlights
- **Focal point:** The peak of the noodle arc mid-lift
- **Composition:** Diagonal lift from lower-right to upper-left, hand entering frame
- **Why:** Motion + hands = humanity. This is the "click here" image.

### Shot 3 — SASHIMI / NIGIRI PLATTER
- **Angle:** 90deg overhead, flat-on
- **Distance:** Medium — full plate plus 15% border
- **Light:** Window light or pendant from directly left, hard shadow side right
- **Focal point:** The front-center piece, tap-lock on the fish flesh
- **Composition:** Plate centered, chopsticks bottom-left diagonal, soy dish top-right — triangle
- **Why:** Overhead sushi is the universal "this place is legit" shot. Proves knife skill and freshness.

### Shot 4 — GYOZA TEXTURE MACRO
- **Angle:** 30deg three-quarter
- **Distance:** Very close — 3-4 gyoza fill frame, rest blurred
- **Light:** Side-back from candle or pendant, raking to catch the crispy bottom skirt
- **Focal point:** The crispy caramelized edge of the front gyoza
- **Composition:** Front gyoza on left third, dipping sauce pooling right third
- **Why:** Texture sells taste. The crispy skirt in sharp focus makes mouths water on mobile screens.

### Shot 5 — YAKITORI / GRILLED SKEWERS
- **Angle:** 20deg, almost side-on
- **Distance:** Medium-close
- **Light:** Backlight from the grill/pass if possible — embers + char glow
- **Focal point:** The charred edge of the front skewer, glistening tare glaze
- **Composition:** Skewers laid diagonal bottom-left to top-right, leading line into frame
- **Why:** Fire, char, glaze. Only shot with warmth in the color palette — balances the cool ramen hero.

### Shot 6 — DONBURI / RICE BOWL (chirashi, katsudon, or unadon)
- **Angle:** 45deg three-quarter
- **Distance:** Medium — bowl + one chopstick resting on rim
- **Light:** Top-left pendant
- **Focal point:** The front edge of the topping where it meets rice
- **Composition:** Bowl left third, empty wood table texture right two-thirds for text
- **Why:** Color variety + composition breathing room. Best candidate for a "menu" section image.

### Shot 7 — SIGNATURE COCKTAIL or MATCHA / TEA POUR
- **Angle:** 0deg flat-on, eye level with the glass
- **Distance:** Close
- **Light:** Backlight through the drink — sit so a pendant or window is directly behind the glass
- **Focal point:** The liquid-ice meniscus or tea foam surface
- **Composition:** Glass centered or left third, bar/table bokeh behind
- **Why:** Backlit liquid = instant premium. Landing page beverage slot + backup hero if food shots fail.

### Shot 8 — ATMOSPHERE / HANDS-IN-FRAME
- **Angle:** 40deg, shooting across the table toward ambient interior
- **Distance:** Wide — table edge, two dishes, a hand reaching, warm pendant bokeh in deep background
- **Light:** Ambient pendants, deliberately underexposed -1.0 for mood
- **Focal point:** The nearest hand or chopstick tip
- **Composition:** Foreground hand lower third, dishes middle third, glowing pendants as bokeh orbs top third
- **Why:** Sells the vibe. Hero image for the "About" or footer section — the feeling of being there.

## 2) iPHONE SETTINGS

- **Live Photo:** OFF. It steals a stop of light and lags.
- **Portrait Mode:** OFF for food. ON only for Shot 8 atmosphere if you want bokeh on hands. Portrait mode fakes depth on flat food and ruins edges of chopsticks and steam.
- **Night Mode:** ON, set to 1 second max (tap the moon icon, slide to 1s, not 3s). Brace hard, exhale, tap shutter, hold phone still for the full second. Night mode beyond 1s smears anything moving — including steam.
- **HDR:** ON (Smart HDR Auto). It saves blown pendant highlights.
- **Grid:** ON. Non-negotiable for overhead and rule-of-thirds.
- **Format:** HEIF Max Quality. ProRAW if you have a Pro model — use it for Shots 1, 3, 7. HEIF for everything else to save time in edit.
- **ProRAW:** ON for the Pro-model hero shots only (1, 3, 7). OFF for the rest — ProRAW files are 25MB+ and slow the shutter.
- **Exposure slider (sun icon):** Tap subject, slide sun DOWN to **-0.3 for matte food, -0.5 for glossy sauces, -0.7 for ramen broth and cocktails**. Protect the highlights or the sauce goes chalky.
- **AE/AF Lock:** Tap and HOLD on the focal point until the yellow "AE/AF LOCK" banner appears. Recompose without losing exposure. Use on every single shot.
- **Lens:** **1x main lens only.** It has the biggest sensor and the widest aperture. 
  - **AVOID the 0.5x ultrawide in this restaurant** — it has a tiny sensor, noisy low-light, distorts plate edges into footballs, and makes food look cheap.
  - 2x/3x telephoto only if you're stuck far from the dish AND there's enough light (which there won't be). Default: 1x, get closer with your feet.

---

## 3) THE ONE RULE

**Put the light source behind the food at 10 or 2 o'clock, never behind you.**

Backlight and side-backlight are what separate restaurant food photos from insurance-claim evidence — they carve texture into noodles, turn steam visible, put a highlight line on every sauce, and make glassware glow. Front light from your own body or the ceiling flattens everything into a mugshot. If you remember nothing else, move your body until the brightest light in the room is behind the plate.

---

## 4) WHAT TO AVOID

- **Flash ON, ever.** The onboard LED is a hard white point-blank nuke that kills every shadow, flattens texture, and makes sauce look like plastic.
- **0.5x ultrawide lens in the dark.** Tiny sensor = noise mush, bent plate edges, amateur tell.
- **Digital zoom past 1x** (unless you have optical 2x/3x). Digital zoom is just cropping — do it in edit, not in camera, so you keep pixels.
- **Mixed color temperature in one frame.** Tungsten pendant + daylight window in the same shot = one side orange, one side blue, unfixable. Pick ONE light source per shot.
- **Shooting through rising steam without a plan.** Steam needs BACKLIGHT to show up — front-lit steam is invisible and just fogs your lens. If you can't backlight, skip the steam shot.
- **Cluttered background.** Menus, phones, ketchup bottles, your beer = death. Clear everything in a 60cm radius before you lift the phone.
- **Bad hand position.** Tense claw hands, knuckles toward camera, nail polish chipping, watch straps. Hands should be relaxed, side-on, fingertips doing the work — chopsticks held halfway up, not at the tip.
- **Cold food / congealed sauce.** You have 90 seconds from plate-down before ramen skins over and cheese hardens. Compose BEFORE the dish arrives so you shoot the instant it hits the table.

## 5) DARK INTERIOR SURVIVAL

**Where to sit:**
- First choice: **window-side booth, subject placed 30cm from the glass**, you sit with the window to your LEFT.
- Second choice: **bar seat at the open kitchen pass** — use the pass lights as backlight.
- Third choice: **directly under a pendant lamp** — pendant becomes your top-back key light.
- Never sit in the dead zone mid-room with only ambient ceiling wash.

**How to brace (no tripod):**
- **Elbows planted on table, both feet flat, phone in two hands.** Thumbs on the side, index fingers free.
- **Phone leaned against a water glass** for overhead shots — the glass is your tripod. Stack sugar packets under the base to micro-adjust angle.
- **Two-hand grip + exhale**: inhale, exhale halfway, hold, tap shutter. Never tap on an inhale.
- For Night Mode 1-second exposures: **press phone edge against a bottle, a menu stand, or the back of the booth.** Any contact point beats none.

**Candle / table light as key:**
- A single tea-light 15-20cm from the dish, placed at 10 o'clock relative to the camera, becomes a warm rim light. Cup your hand behind it to stop lens flare.
- Dial exposure to **-0.5** and let the shadows go black. Moody beats muddy.

**When to ask the server to move the dish:**
- If the plate lands in the dead zone, politely ask: *"Sorry, could we slide this 30cm toward the window for a photo?"* Do it before they walk away. Takes 4 seconds. Always works.

**Exposure compensation dial-in (numbers, not vibes):**
- Ramen broth, glossy sauce, cocktail with ice: **-0.7**
- Sushi, sashimi, yakitori glaze: **-0.5**
- Gyoza, rice bowls, matte dishes: **-0.3**
- Atmosphere Shot 8: **-1.0** for mood
- Never go positive. Highlights blown = shot dead.

## 6) BACKUP PLAN

**Fallback order if a shot fails:**
1. **Reshoot same dish from a new angle** — switch from 25deg to 90deg overhead, or overhead to side-on. New angle, same food.
2. **Swap to detail crop** — if the full dish looks wrong, go macro on one element (one gyoza, one nigiri, one noodle cluster). A sharp detail beats a mediocre wide.
3. **Substitute with atmosphere shot** — if food is dead (cold, ugly plating, congealed), shoot hands + pendants + bokeh instead. Mood saves the slot.
4. **Cocktail / tea hero as filler** — the backlit drink (Shot 7) is your universal rescue image. It fits almost any landing page slot.

**MINIMUM VIABLE 4 SHOTS — if the clock kills you, NAIL THESE:**
1. **Shot 1 — Ramen hero** (the anchor, non-negotiable)

These four cover hero / variety / texture / atmosphere. If you get these, the landing page ships. Everything else is bonus.
